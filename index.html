Our Celebration Guide వేడుక మార్గదర్శి

Never been to a South Indian Hindu wedding? You're in for something beautiful. Here's what to expect at each celebration — so you can be fully present in every moment.

The Haldi is a joyful, informal pre-wedding celebration held the day before the wedding. "Haldi" means turmeric — and in Telugu tradition it is called pasupu. Family and friends gather to apply a paste of turmeric, sandalwood, and rose water to the bride and groom's face, arms, and feet.

Why turmeric?

In Hindu tradition, turmeric is sacred. It is believed to purify the body, protect from negative energy, and give the skin a natural glow before the wedding. Applying it is both a blessing and a celebration.

What will happen

  • The couple sit on decorated seats surrounded by flowers
  • Family and close friends take turns applying the turmeric paste to the couple's face and hands
  • Traditional songs are sung and music is played — it's loud, joyful, and lively
  • Yellow flowers and marigolds are everywhere — this is the color of the day
  • Photography and videos are encouraged and celebrated

What to wear

Wear yellow and orange — these are the colors of the day. Casual and comfortable is the goal, since turmeric stains and silk doesn't mix well. Leave your white, cream, and light-colored clothes at home for this one.

Women: A salwar kameez or anarkali in yellow or orange, a cotton or chiffon saree, or simply a bright yellow sundress or maxi. Avoid silk sarees — turmeric will stain them permanently.

Men: A kurta pajama in yellow, mustard, or orange, or casual linen pants and a collared shirt. Simple and comfortable is perfect.

💛 Tip: Bring your phone fully charged — this ceremony has incredible photo moments. And don't worry if you get some turmeric on you; it's considered a blessing!

A traditional Telugu Hindu wedding ceremony is a sacred, ancient ritual conducted by a Hindu priest (called a pandit) in Sanskrit and Telugu. The ceremony typically lasts 2–3 hours and is rich with symbolism, prayer, and meaning. Rather than a performance, each ritual is a spiritual act — the couple making vows before God, family, and the sacred fire.

Key moments to watch for

  • Ganesh Puja — The ceremony opens with prayers to Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles, seeking blessings for the union.
  • Vara Puja — The groom is welcomed with prayers and garlands by the bride's family.
  • Kasi Yatra — A playful moment: the groom pretends to leave for a life of solitude (like a monk), and the bride's brother convinces him to stay and marry instead. It draws a lot of laughter.
  • Kanyadan — The most sacred moment. The bride's father formally places her hand in the groom's, giving his daughter in marriage. This is often deeply emotional for families.
  • Mangalsutra — The groom ties a sacred gold necklace around the bride's neck — this is the Telugu wedding's equivalent of exchanging rings. She will wear it for life.
  • Talambralu — The bride and groom pour rice mixed with turmeric over each other's heads. It's joyful and a little playful — there is always laughter.
  • Saptapadi (Seven Steps) — The couple walks seven steps together around the sacred fire, making seven vows. This is the spiritual heart and legal core of the marriage.
  • Jeelakarra Bellam — The couple apply a cumin and jaggery paste to each other's heads — a ritual symbolizing the "tying" of their hearts together.

What to do as a guest

  • Sit quietly and observe — the rituals are being performed in Sanskrit and Telugu
  • Feel free to ask a family member near you to explain what's happening at any point
  • You will be invited to bless the couple by throwing flower petals — follow the lead of those around you
  • Please keep phones put away during the ceremony — our photographer has everything covered

What to wear

The wedding ceremony calls for formal attire. Bright, festive colors are encouraged — gold, red, deep blue, emerald green, and jewel tones are all beautiful. Please avoid white or black, as these colors carry specific significance in Indian tradition.

Women (Indian attire): A Kanchipuram or Kanjeevaram silk saree is the most traditional choice and fits the ceremony beautifully. A lehenga choli (full skirt, blouse, and dupatta) or a formal anarkali suit are equally stunning. All three options look spectacular for ceremony photos.

Men (Indian attire): A sherwani (long embroidered coat) is the most formal option and perfect for the ceremony. A dhoti kurta (traditional draped lower garment with kurta) is very traditional and often worn by family. A kurta pajama with a Nehru jacket is a comfortable and polished choice.

Western formal is equally welcome: A suit and tie (men) or a floor-length gown or formal cocktail dress in a rich, festive color (women). You'll fit right in and look wonderful.

🌺 Not sure what to wear? A suit and tie (men) or formal dress (women) in a bold, festive color is always perfect — and very welcomed. The most important thing is that you feel comfortable and celebrate with us.

The reception is the evening celebration where both families and all guests come together to celebrate the newlyweds. Think of it as a beautiful blend of Indian and American wedding traditions — dinner, toasts, music, and a whole lot of dancing.

What to expect

  • Cocktail hour with drinks and appetizers as guests arrive
  • Grand entrance of the wedding party and the couple
  • Welcome speeches and toasts from family
  • A traditional South Indian feast — expect an abundance of incredible food including both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes
  • First dance and special family dances
  • Open dance floor — and yes, Bollywood and Telugu film music will be played. The dance floor will be packed.
  • Cake cutting and desserts

What to wear

Semi-formal to formal. The reception is more relaxed than the ceremony — celebratory and festive is the goal. You'll want something you can actually dance in, because the dance floor will be packed all night.

Women (Indian attire): A lighter chiffon or georgette saree is great for dancing. A lehenga choli or anarkali that you're comfortable moving in are excellent choices. Indo-western styles (fusion dresses, palazzo sets) are also popular and perfectly appropriate.

Men (Indian attire): A kurta pajama is ideal — more comfortable for dancing than a sherwani. A Nehru jacket over a kurta elevates the look while keeping things relaxed. Many men wear their ceremony outfit for the reception as well.

Western semi-formal is equally welcome: A cocktail dress or elegant midi for women, a blazer or sport coat for men. Wear comfortable shoes — or bring a pair to change into.

Indian wedding etiquette tips

  • It is customary to greet the couple's parents and elders first — a simple "Namaste" with hands pressed together is always appreciated
  • Shoes may be removed in certain ceremonial areas — look for a shoe rack or follow the lead of others
  • When in doubt about any custom, just ask — our family loves sharing the traditions
  • Enjoy the food enthusiastically — a South Indian feast is meant to be savored and seconds are expected!
🎶 Pro tip: Wear comfortable shoes you can dance in, or bring a pair to change into. Indian wedding receptions are known for their incredible dancing — and you will absolutely be pulled onto the floor.
