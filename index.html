Our Celebration Guide వేడుక మార్గదర్శి

The Haldi is our joyful pre-wedding celebration with turmeric, flowers, music, and a lot of family energy. It is bright, playful, and one of the most personal parts of the weekend.

What to expect

  • Turmeric blessings from family and close friends
  • Music, laughter, and a little friendly family competition
  • Lots of yellow, marigolds, and photo moments
Wear something comfortable in yellow or orange, and do not worry about a little turmeric. For outfit ideas, see Dress Code.

Our wedding ceremony is a traditional Telugu Hindu ceremony led by a priest, with blessings, rituals, and a few playful moments woven in. Even if the prayers are unfamiliar, you will still be able to feel the meaning and joy in the room.

What to expect

  • Prayers, garlands, and family blessings
  • Signature Telugu moments like Talambralu and the seven steps
  • A mix of sacred rituals and lighthearted moments
During the ceremony, we would love for you to stay present and keep phones tucked away. For what to wear, see Dress Code.

The reception is our evening celebration with dinner, toasts, music, and a full dance floor. It will feel festive, welcoming, and a little bit like the best kind of family party.

What to expect

  • Cocktail hour, dinner, and family toasts
  • A mix of Indian and American wedding traditions
  • Bollywood, Telugu, and crowd-favorite dance music
Wear something festive that you can move in, and come ready to dance. More details are in Dress Code.

Dress Code దుస్తుల నియమాలు

Festive colors are always welcome. We especially recommend yellow, orange, and white across the weekend.

August 15 · Haldi

Recommended colors: Yellow, orange, and white.

Ladies: Sundresses, sarees, lehengas, shararas, or chudidhars.

Gentlemen: Cotton or linen pants with shirts or kurtas.

Keep it light, comfortable, and easy to move in. Haldi is one of the most joyful parts of the weekend.
August 16 · Ceremony

Recommended look: Formal and festive.

Ladies: Silk saree, lehenga, formal anarkali, floor-length gown, or cocktail dress.

Gentlemen: Sherwani, dhoti kurta, kurta with a Nehru jacket, or a suit and tie.

If you are unsure, a formal outfit in a rich festive color is always a great choice.
August 16 · Reception

Recommended look: Semi-formal and festive.

Ladies: Saree, lehenga, anarkali, cocktail dress, or a dress you can comfortably dance in.

Gentlemen: Kurta, blazer, sport coat, or a polished semi-formal look.

Comfort matters here. The dance floor will be busy, so wear something you can enjoy the night in.
